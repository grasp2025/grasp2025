GRASP: Advancing Energy-Efficient Solutions for RF Sensing and Communication.

September 1-4 • Istanbul, Türkiye

Workshop at IEEE PIMRC 2025 in Istanbul, Türkiye

ABOUT

This workshop will explore the transformative potential of AI in reshaping RF sensing, communication, and energy-efficient systems for next-generation wireless networks. By focusing on the integration of AI with advanced RF technologies, the workshop aims to address key challenges such as optimizing sensing processes, reducing energy consumption, and enabling seamless communication in dynamic and/or resource-constrained environments. On-device intelligence will be an important topic, showcasing how lightweight AI models enable real-time data processing and decision-making directly on resource-constrained devices, drastically reducing latency and energy consumption. Wi-Fi sensing will also be highlighted, with AI augmenting its potential for applications such as activity recognition, localization, and environmental monitoring. Additionally, this workshop will emphasize joint sensing and communication frameworks that unify these processes for resource efficiency and scalability, supported by edge computing and AI-driven orchestration.

CALL FOR PAPERS

This workshop aims to promote the development of distributed learning approaches to improve the scalability and efficiency of RF systems while maintaining privacy in the system. To bridge theory and practice, the workshop will feature insights from physical experiments validating AI-enabled RF sensing and communication solutions in diverse real-world scenarios.

The main topics of interests include (but are not limited to):

  1. AI-Driven RF Sensing and Communication Optimization
  2. Efficient ML Solutions for On-Device Processing/Learning
  3. Reinforcement Learning for Real-Time Sensing and Communication
  4. Joint Communication and Sensing frameworks
  5. Wi-Fi Sensing empowered with AI Techniques
  6. Distributed Learning for RF Systems
  7. Physical Experimentation and Validation of AI Solutions
  8. AI-Enhanced Resource Management for RF Environments
  9. AI-driven RF sensing for Human Activity Recognition (HAR)

SUBMISSION GUIDELINES

We follow the same regular paper submission guidelines as the main track of the conference. All final submissions of accepted papers must be written in English with a maximum paper length of six (6) printed pages (10-point font) including figures. No more than one (1) additional printed page (10-point font) may be included in final submissions and the extra page (the 7th page) will incur an over length page charge of US$100.

To prepare your manuscript, use the formatting guidelines provided in the: IEEE PIMRC Submission Guidelines.
